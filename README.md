# 🦠 California Infectious Disease Surveillance Dashboard (2014–2023)


## 📊 Project Overview
This project presents a longitudinal analysis of infectious disease trends in California over a ten-year period. By transforming raw surveillance data from the California Department of Public Health (CDPH) into an interactive dashboard, this tool identifies geographic hotspots, demographic disparities, and temporal shifts in public health.

The goal is to provide epidemiologists and public health officials with a clear view of disease burden, shifting the focus from simple case counts to population-adjusted incidence rates for more effective resource allocation.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
- Excel: Data cleaning and standardization of county-level reporting.
- Power BI: Advanced data visualization, geospatial mapping, and trend analysis.

  
## 💡 Data Insights
- The 2023 Surge: After a significant decline during the 2020 pandemic period (likely due to shifted healthcare focus and reporting delays), case counts reached a 10-year peak in 2023, hitting an average rate of 207 per 100K.
- Geographic Hotspots: While high-population centers like Los Angeles show high total volumes, Kern County emerged as the critical hotspot for the highest Incidence Rate, identifying it as a priority zone for regional intervention.
- Environmental Drivers: Analysis of diseases like Coccidioidomycosis (Valley Fever) shows a notable male-to-female disparity (81K vs 54K), suggesting occupational and environmental exposure (outdoor labor) as a primary transmission driver.
- Demographic Parity: Several enteric diseases (like Campylobacteriosis) show near-parity across genders, indicating that community-wide environmental factors are more influential than individual demographic behaviors.
- Efficiency in Reporting: Despite the rising counts, the -10% YoY Change in recent reporting efficiency suggests improved state-wide surveillance and data capture.
